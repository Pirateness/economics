Subsides

Subsides are cash payments made to local producers to increase supply in the face of import competition.

The effect of a subsidy is a movement in quantity produced from Qe to Q1 as price decreases from Pe to P1. This is a result of increased supply, as inefficient firms are now able to produce more of a good for a reduced cost.

Quotas

Quotas are quantitative restrictions on certain categories of imported goods. The larger the import quota, the greater the quantity of goods that can be imported. The smaller the import quota, the lesser quantity of goods that can be imported. A quota reduces supply of imports available in the market, pushing up import prices, meaning that inefficient import competing industries are still able to compete.

The effect of a quota is shown. Without the quota foriegn firms supply the difference between Q1 and Q2, as domestic firms only supply Q1 at the world price (pw). With a quota the supply of goods produced by foriegn firms contracts to Q3 and Q4 as the world price (pw) increases to pw+quota. As a result domestic firms are able to produce Q3 at the new world price, while foriegn firms are able to produce Q4.
